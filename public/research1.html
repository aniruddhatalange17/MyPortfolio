Introduction

Branching Design:

  • Developed bio-inspired branching algorithms for improved fluid flow and thermal performance in engineering applications.
  • Studied biological laws governing branching structures, including Murray's Law, Horton's Law, and Leonardo's Law.

Simulation:

  • Conducted mesh refinement studies to optimize simulation accuracy and efficiency.
  • Compared results from ANSYS Fluent with Flownex software for validation.

Additive Manufacturing:

  • Experimented with Polyjet and Selective Laser Sintering 3D printing for creating branching channels.
  • Printed symmetric and organic designs with varying channel diameters.
  • Tuned ECU with Ricardo and Powertronic software (RACE DYNAMICS) KIT for optimal engine performance

Experimental validation:

  • Created a flow visualization setup to observe fluid behavior in branching channels.
  • Planned experiments to compare flow rate predictions with simulation results.
  • Executed a Design Verification Plan & Report (DVP&R) and Design Failure Mode & Effects Analysis (DFMEA) to identify and mitigate potential failures

Simulation Methodology

  • Utilized Rhino and Grasshopper software to create initial 3D
  • Tackled the challenge of non-uniform flow distribution in a multi-geometry.
  • Performed necessary cleanup operations in SpaceClaim to ensure model integrity.
  • Applied inflation layers over the outlets to accurately capture fluid behavior.
  • Converted to Fluent meshing and Implemented hexagonal tetrahedral meshing for improved accuracy.
  • Generated contour plots for comprehensive flow visualization.
  • Created volume flow rendering plots to identify key result areas.
  • Studied mass flow rate averages across outlets.
  • Analyzed pressure results throughout the system.

Results:

  • Tackled the challenge of non-uniform flow distribution in a multi-geometry.
  • Mastered meshing techniques to ensure accurate CFD simulation results, particularly at the model outlets.
  • Successfully achieved uniform mass flow rate across all outlets, demonstrating significant improvement from the initial centralized flow pattern.
  • Developed an innovative flow visualization system that automates meshing and generates Excel-based reports for various designs.
  • Streamlined design optimization process by efficiently analyzing mesh quality and predicting uniform flow, reducing analysis time by approximately 25%.
